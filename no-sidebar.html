University of Southampton Lunar Hopper Project

Lead Engineer

A prototype Lunar VTVL rover, powered by a 400N hybrid rocket motor.

The Lunar Hopper Mk. II is a vertical takeoff, vertical landing (VTVL) vehicle that has been built to demonstrate an alternative mode of lunar transport, and is currently being developed as a partnership between the University of Southampton and the Spaceflight Society. A Hopper would be deployed to the surface of the moon, where it can identify potential areas of interest to explore. It would then boost up, pitch over, and translate to its selected destination. The Hopper is completely terrain independent and capable of travelling long distances faster than conventional space land vehicles.

Weighing in at 37 kilograms, the Hopper encompasses various engineering systems to ensure it is successful through all phases of flight. They include the Attitude Control System (ACS), Propellant Delivery System (PDS), Structure, Software and Electronics, Communications, and the independent pilot trainer.

The Hopper is propelled by a 400 Newton hybrid rocket motor that was developed at the University of Southampton. It maintains stability and control using four nitrogen cold gas thrusters located at the top of the ACS tower.

My involvement

I lead and participate in fast-paced, concurrent design of control systems, propulsion systems, structures, electronics and the development of software, as well as guiding the project to keep to financial and time restrictions. I am currently navigating the project through securing funding to modify the Hopper ready for flight, as well as leading the design and manufacture of replacement parts.
